"During the Civil War Goshen furnished 24 men in excess of its quota of 122. In 1861 the population of Goshen was thirteen-hundred, which meant that one out of every nine persons-man, woman and child-was a soldier. Again, not all were patriotic." "On June 23, 1861, a rebel flag was said to be flying at a residence on East Street. After some earnest persuasion the flag was taken down. Thereupon, a committee was sent to the legislature to push through a law making the raising of a secessionist flag a criminal offense in the state of Connecticut."

From: GOSHEN CONNECTICUT a town above all others

Goshen is a much divided town these days. Over half of Goshenites voted to retain the former president as their leader. Some continue to display the signs, flags and paraphernalia in support of him. They are our neighbors, friends, relatives who live and serve in our community. While we can not support their ideas of sedition and lawlessness, we have to find a way to reach out to them. Why are they so willing to disregard our Constitution, and to deny truth? Our goal is to try to find common ground on the things we agree on to move Goshen to a more prosperous future while retaining our rural look. It is also to continue our great history to be the leaders in keeping our Democratic Republic a transparent, open government supporting the ideals of Liberty, Freedom and equal justice under the law.
